Who am I?

A tricky question, indeed. At the moment I'm a web developer who's looking for place to sharpen his skills. I've just finished General Assembly's immersive web development course, where I've focused among other things, on Ruby-on-Rails, Javascript, jQuery and implementing APIs.


Before I made the move to towards coding I spent five years working as a technician at Flowers Gallery, in London. This involved working with artists to bring their work from the studio, through the cataloguing process, to be installed at exhibitions or art fairs around London and Europe, before finally being hung in a collector's home.


Thinking that it was high time to strike some items off the bucket-list, in April 2013 I decided to up and leave for Thailand to sample life as an English teacher. I started out in village called Wangsaphung, in the northeastern province of Loei. Here I spent six months exploring the mountains, caves and rice paddies by motorbike, fishing on a friend's farm, eating and speaking Thai, and of course teaching a spot of English.

After leaving Wangsaphung, I travelled for three months through Laos, Vietnam and Cambodia, before arriving back in Bangkok. I remained here for a year, teaching at Vajravudh College; an elite, boys boarding school, and rather different from my experience in the northeast.

A year and a half in Asia gave me the opportunity to indulge my love of travelling, to meet a lot of great of people and get myself into all kinds of interesting situations...
